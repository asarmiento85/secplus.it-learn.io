# 5.3 — Third-Party Risk Assessment and Management

## What you need to know

Third-party risk management addresses the security risks introduced when organizations share data, systems, or processes with vendors, suppliers, and partners. The exam places heavy emphasis on the different agreement types — you must know what each one is used for and be able to distinguish between SLA, MOA, MOU, MSA, SOW, NDA, and BPA. You also need to understand vendor assessment methods (penetration testing, right-to-audit, SOC reports) and the ongoing monitoring obligations after a contract is signed. Agreement type questions are among the most frequently tested in Domain 5.

---

## Why Third-Party Risk Matters

- Organizations rarely operate in isolation — they depend on cloud providers, SaaS vendors, contractors, and supply chain partners
- **Your vendor's security failure can become your breach** — Target breach originated through an HVAC vendor
- **Supply chain attacks** target vendors to reach their customers (e.g., SolarWinds)
- You remain responsible for the security of data shared with third parties
- Due diligence before signing and ongoing monitoring after signing are both required

---

## Vendor Assessment Methods

Before and during a vendor relationship, organizations use multiple methods to evaluate vendor security posture.

| Method | Description | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **Penetration Testing** | Security testing of vendor systems to find exploitable vulnerabilities | Pre-contract due diligence or annual requirement |
| **Right-to-Audit Clause** | Contract provision giving the customer the right to audit vendor security practices | Included in contract; exercised periodically or after incidents |
| **Evidence of Internal Audits** | Vendor provides SOC 2 reports, audit logs, or compliance documentation | Ongoing monitoring; annual review |
| **Independent Third-Party Assessment** | External assessors evaluate vendor (e.g., ISO 27001 certification audit) | Pre-contract or renewal |
| **Supply Chain Analysis** | Examining the security of the vendor's own vendors (n-th party risk) | Critical for software supply chain, hardware components |
| **Security Questionnaires** | Standardized forms asking about vendor controls (CAIQ, SIG, VSAQ) | Initial vendor selection, annual review |

### Key Questionnaire Standards

| Questionnaire | Used By |
|---|---|
| **CAIQ** (Consensus Assessment Initiative Questionnaire) | Cloud vendors; published by Cloud Security Alliance (CSA) |
| **SIG** (Standardized Information Gathering) | Shared Assessments program; broad enterprise vendor use |
| **VSAQ** (Vendor Security Assessment Questionnaire) | Google's open-source template; widely adapted |

## Vendor Selection Process

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| **Due Diligence** | Research the vendor before signing — review SOC reports, certifications, financial stability, reputation |
| **Conflict of Interest Check** | Ensure the vendor evaluating or auditing you has no financial relationship with the assessed party |
| **Risk Scoring** | Assign inherent risk tier based on data access and service criticality |
| **Contract Negotiation** | Include security requirements, right-to-audit, breach notification obligations |
| **Onboarding** | Validate security controls before granting access; minimum privilege |

## Agreement Types

This is the most tested area of 5.3. Know the purpose and distinguishing features of every agreement type.

| Agreement | Full Name | Binding? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement | Yes | Defines performance standards (uptime, response times) and remedies for failure to meet them |
| **MOA** | Memorandum of Agreement | Yes | Formal binding agreement between two parties detailing roles and responsibilities |
| **MOU** | Memorandum of Understanding | No | Non-binding document expressing intent to work together; no legal enforcement |
| **MSA** | Master Service Agreement | Yes | Umbrella contract establishing terms that govern multiple projects or engagements |
| **SOW / Work Order** | Statement of Work | Yes | Specific deliverables, timeline, and costs for a particular project (used under an MSA) |
| **NDA** | Non-Disclosure Agreement | Yes | Obligates parties to keep shared information confidential |
| **BPA** | Business Partnership Agreement | Yes | Defines the terms and conditions of a business partnership |

### Key Distinctions

- **MOU vs. MOA**: MOU is non-binding (letters of intent); MOA is binding (actual obligation)
- **MSA vs. SOW**: MSA is the umbrella framework; SOW is the specific task order under that framework
- **SLA** is performance-focused — it defines uptime guarantees and financial penalties for missing targets
- **NDA** is confidentiality-focused — it protects sensitive information shared during the relationship
- **BPA** governs the overall business relationship — profit sharing, liability, IP ownership

### SLA Key Elements

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Uptime Guarantee** | 99.9% uptime = ~8.7 hours downtime/year; 99.99% = ~52 minutes/year |
| **Response Time** | How quickly the vendor must respond to incidents or service degradation |
| **Remedies** | Credits, refunds, or penalties when SLA is breached |
| **Reporting** | How and when the vendor reports performance metrics |
| **Termination Clause** | Rights to exit contract if SLA is repeatedly missed |

## Vendor Monitoring

Signing a contract is not the end of vendor risk management — ongoing oversight is required.

| Activity | Description |
|---|---|
| **Performance Monitoring** | Track SLA metrics; review monthly/quarterly dashboards |
| **Security Reviews** | Annual review of vendor SOC 2 reports, vulnerability scan results |
| **Patch Status Review** | Verify vendor applies patches to systems with access to your environment |
| **Access Reviews** | Quarterly review of what vendor accounts can access and whether access is still needed |
| **Incident Notification** | Vendors must notify you of breaches involving your data within contract-specified timeframe |
| **Contract Renewal Review** | Reassess risk and update requirements at each renewal |

---

## Rules of Engagement

- **Rules of engagement** define what is permitted during security assessments (penetration testing, red team exercises)
- Specify: target systems in scope, excluded systems, allowed techniques, testing window (hours), notification requirements
- Must be agreed to in writing before any testing begins
- Prevents legal liability and ensures assessors don't disrupt production systems

## Supply Chain Risk

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Software Supply Chain** | Malicious code inserted into open-source libraries or vendor updates | SBOM (Software Bill of Materials), signed updates |
| **Hardware Supply Chain** | Counterfeit or tampered hardware components | Trusted vendor lists, hardware attestation |
| **Cloud Dependency** | Vendor relies on another cloud provider that may have its own risks | Review vendor's sub-processors; include in risk assessment |
| **Geographic Risk** | Vendor operates in or data transits through high-risk jurisdictions | Data sovereignty review; contract data location requirements |

## Exam Tips

- **MOU is non-binding** — it expresses intent but creates no legal obligation; MOA is binding
- **SLA** focuses on performance and uptime guarantees with financial remedies for failures
- **MSA** is the master framework; **SOW** (Statement of Work) defines a specific project under the MSA
- **NDA** protects confidentiality of information shared between parties — critical before sharing sensitive data
- **Right-to-audit clause** gives you the contractual right to audit a vendor — it must be negotiated upfront
- **SOC 2 Type II** is stronger than Type I — Type II covers controls over a 6–12 month period; Type I is a point-in-time snapshot
- **Due diligence** = research before signing; **Due care** = ongoing reasonable protective actions after signing
- Supply chain attacks exploit vendor relationships — attackers compromise the vendor to reach the customer
- **CAIQ** is the Cloud Security Alliance questionnaire — used specifically for cloud vendor assessments
- Vendor monitoring is ongoing — signing the contract does not end your third-party risk obligations
